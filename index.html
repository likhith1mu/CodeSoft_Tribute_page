A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Dr. Kalam is widely known as the "People's President" due to his deep connection with the citizens of India, especially the youth. Kalam's early life was marked by a modest upbringing, but he possessed an insatiable curiosity and a passion for learning. He graduated in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and went on to pursue advanced studies in the United States. He contributed significantly to India's space and military programs, notably his pivotal role in the development of the country's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle and ballistic missile systems. What set Dr. Kalam apart was not just his scientific achievements but also his commitment to education and his vision for a better India. He believed in harnessing the potential of the country's youth and often spoke to students, encouraging them to dream big and work hard. His books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," have inspired countless young Indians. In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the President of India, becoming one of the most beloved and admired heads of state in the nation's history. He used his presidency to further promote education, innovation, and self-reliance. APJ Abdul Kalam's legacy lives on as a symbol of India's scientific prowess, a source of inspiration for its youth, and a reminder of the power of humility and dedication. His life and work continue to inspire people not only in India but around the world, making him an iconic figure in the fields of science, education, and public service.
